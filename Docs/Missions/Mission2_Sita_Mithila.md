# Mission 2: The Earth Furrow of Mithila

*   **Playable Protagonist:** Princess Sita (Playable Childhood & Swayamvara States)
*   **Reveals & Transitions to:** King Dasharatha (Palace Chambers, Ayodhya)
*   **Theme & Focus:** Pure Earth alignment, divine grace, maternal strength, and the tragic transition from royal prosperity to ascetic exile.

## 2. Basic Flow of Steps

1.  **Childhood Prologue:** Playable child Sita in Janaka's palace gardens. The player learns basic movement, crop-blessing mechanics, and earth-aura manipulation.
2.  **Sacred Garden Defense (Monster Hunt):** Clear the palace orchard of invading *Bhumisuras* (Asuric subterranean burrowers) that are drying up the roots of the holy Tulsi plants.
3.  **The Pinaka Sanctuary Puzzle:** Solve the ancient Earth-Tuning puzzle to unlock the temple gates and casually lift the massive Pinaka chest to retrieve a lost ball.
4.  **Time Jump & The Swayamvara:** Shifting to the adult Princess Sita. The player oversees the ceremony as dozens of kings fail to lift the bow.
5.  **Rama's Triumph:** A cinematic QTE sequence where Prince Rama lifts, strings, and breaks the celestial *Pinaka* bow, sending a massive shockwave across Mithila.
6.  **Boss Encounter (Sage Parashurama):** Surrendering the broken bow's pieces, the player must endure the wrath of Parashurama, shielding the court using Sita's earth barriers until Rama pacifies him.
7.  **The Transition Nerf (Valkala Swap):** After traveling to Ayodhya, Kaikeyi's exile decree strikes. Sita, Rama, and Lakshmana must shed all royal armor, weapons, and accessories, swapping them for raw bark robes, permanently capping health/defense stats.
8.  **Reveal:** Dasharatha, heartbroken, locks himself in the Chamber of Grief. The player's camera shifts to control King Dasharatha as the palace plunges into sorrow.

## 3. Fun Monster Hunting

### Purifying the Bhumisuras (Subterranean Nibblers)
*   **Mechanic:** Subterranean pests are eating the roots of the sacred grove. They move underground, revealed only by shifting soil trails, and pop up to spit poisonous mud.
*   **Combat Flow:** Sita cannot deal direct damage through violence. Instead, she uses her **Earth Aura Pulse** (Bhumi Spandan) to ripple the ground, forcing the creatures to the surface. Once exposed, she throws **Sacred Ganga-Jal (Holy Water)** on them, purifying their Asuric forms and converting them into glowing, harmless forest rabbits that run away.
*   **Enemy Type:** *Bhumisuras* — Blind, rock-skinned gophers that burrow rapidly and resist physical blows, vulnerable only to pure spiritual elemental alignment.

---

## 4. Fun Puzzle Solving

### The Pinaka Chest Earth-Tuning (Bhumi Gati)
*   **Context:** As a young child, Sita's ball rolls underneath the colossal iron chest containing Shiva's Bow, *Pinaka*. The chest is anchored to the earth by ancient gravity seals.
*   **Puzzle Mechanics:**
    *   The room contains three large circular floor-seals, each representing an elemental axis of the Earth: *Gati* (Rotation), *Sthira* (Stability), and *Akasha* (Spiritual Weight).
    *   The player must guide glowing green ley-line energy from the walls into the floor seals by rotating marble prisms.
    *   Once the ley lines are aligned, the gravity weight of the chest drops to zero. Child Sita can then casually push the massive, million-ton chest aside with one hand, retrieving her ball, showing her innate connection to the Earth mother (*Bhumidevi*).

## 5. Boss Encounters

### Boss 1: Sage Parashurama (The Avatar of the Axe)
*   **Combat Arena:** The ruined Swayamvara Courtyard, cracked and burning with Parashurama's golden celestial fury.
*   **Boss Mechanics:**
    *   *Phase 1 (The Aura Shield):* Parashurama launches supersonic shockwaves with his battleaxe (*Parashu*). Playing as Sita, the player must place protective mud-domes and stone shields to guard her father, King Janaka, and the frightened citizens from collateral damage.
    *   *Phase 2 (The Bow of Vishnu):* Parashurama challenges Rama to string the *Sharanga* (Vishnu's Bow). The player must switch to supporting Rama, casting earth-resonance buffs to stabilize his footing against Parashurama's crushing gravitational pressure field.
    *   *Defeat Condition:* The battle ends not by killing the boss, but by Rama successfully stringing the bow and proving his own divine nature, causing Parashurama to bow in respect and leave.

## 6. The Character Nerf & Transition

### The Ascetic Strip (Valkala Swap)
*   **The Decree:** Queen Kaikeyi demands Rama's 14-year banishment. Sita and Lakshmana vow to go with him.
*   **The Nerf Mechanics (The Valkala Swap):**
    *   At the borders of Ayodhya, the royal retinue is dismissed. A cinematic triggers where Sita and Rama voluntarily remove their golden crowns, silk garments, and celestial jewel-encrusted armor.
    *   They equip the **Valkala (Coarse Bark) Robes**.
    *   *Mechanical Impact:*
        *   **Health Cap:** Max HP is permanently reduced by 50% for the forest acts.
        *   **Defense Lock:** Standard physical defense is reduced to near-zero. No heavy iron armor can be equipped.
        *   **Weapon Lock:** Sita's high-tier gold-dust flasks and celestial energy charms are locked away in the Ayodhya vault, leaving her with basic forest flowers and herbs.
*   **Character Swap:** The camera pans back from the dusty chariot tracks of the departing trio. It enters the dark palace chambers, zooming onto the weeping face of King Dasharatha, who lies paralyzed on his bed, gasping for breath. The player takes control of King Dasharatha.

## 8. Weapons & Equipment Used

*   **Bhumi Spandan (Earth Pulse):** Sita's primary ability to trigger ground ripples, reveal burrowing enemies, and activate ancient temple seals.
*   **Ganga-Jal Flask (Ritual Water):** Used to purify corrupt elemental creatures and cleanse Asuric spores in the environment.
*   **Valkala Garments (Equipped State):** Coarse bark clothing that imposes a severe defense penalty but grants a minor buff to forest camouflage and stealth tailing.
