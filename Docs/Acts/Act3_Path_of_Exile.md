# Act 3: The Path of Exile
*   **Traditional Reference:** Ayodhya Kanda (Chapters 1 - 119)
*   **Act Focus:** The palace intrigue of Manthara and Kaikeyi, acceptance of the 14-year exile, crossing the Ganges river with Guha's aid, settling in Chitrakoot, and Bharata's emotional visit to claim Rama's sandals (*Paduka*).

---

## 1. Overview & Narrative Flow
On the eve of Rama's coronation as Crown Prince, the elderly King Dasharatha's mind is forced by Queen Kaikeyi, instigated by her maid Manthara. Kaikeyi demands two boons promised to her long ago: that her son Bharata be crowned king, and that Rama be exiled to the dangerous Dandaka forest for fourteen years. 

To preserve his father's honor and the sanctity of truth (*Satya*), Rama accepts the decree immediately. Sita and Lakshmana refuse to let him go alone, joining him. The royal trio leaves behind their wealth, changing into bark-cloth. They cross the holy Ganges river with the help of the boatman chief Guha and build a peaceful hermitage in Chitrakoot. Bharata tracks them down to beg Rama to return, but Rama refuses to break his father's promise. Bharata returns with Rama's sandals to rule only as a regent.

---

## 2. Moral Theme
*   **Satya (Uncompromising Truth) & Pitrudharma (Filial Duty):** Rama teaches that keeping an oath is the highest honor. Personal ambition must be sacrificed if it compromises ethical vows.

---

## 3. Active Characters & Objectives
*   **Lord Rama (Playable):** Navigate the palace chambers, defuse palace guards peacefully, accept exile, cross the Ganges, and establish Chitrakoot camp.
*   **Sita (Companion NPC):** Refuse safety and choose to accompany Rama, healing team members during the river crossing hazards.
*   **Lakshmana (Playable Skirmisher):** Clear path hazards, build the leaf cottage (*Parnakuti*) in Chitrakoot, and defend the camp.
*   **Queen Kaikeyi & Manthara (Dialogue Boss):** Present toxic verbal arguments that the player must counter by choosing righteous options (Dharmic Dialogues).
*   **Prince Bharata (Companion NPC):** Intercept Rama in Chitrakoot, offer his allegiance, and accept the wooden sandals.

---

## 4. World & Locations
*   **Ayodhya Palace Chambers (Darkness/Shadow Theme):** Once bright, now shrouded in dark shadows, weeping citizens, and tense guard encounters.
*   **The Ganges River Bank (Shrungiverapura):** A wide, misty river crossing with heavy currents, reeds, and wooden rafts.
*   **Chitrakoot Mountain:** A peaceful, green hill filled with singing birds, clear streams, and rocky cliffs.

---

## 5. Act Boss Battle: The Web of Oaths (Dialogue & Escape Boss)
*   **Boss Character:** This act features a non-lethal "Dialogue and Escape" encounter representing palace politics.
*   **Phase 1 (The Palace Debate):** Rama is summoned to Kaikeyi's chamber. The player faces a barrage of verbal traps. Drawing a sword results in a fail; the player must choose options emphasizing *Dharma*, *Satya*, and *Pity* (fatherly duty), which gradually drains Kaikeyi's stubborn argument gauge.
*   **Phase 2 (The Tearful Escape):** The citizens block Rama's path, begging him to stay. The player must navigate through the city crowds using stealth and diversion tactics without harming any citizen.
*   **Phase 3 (Crossing the Ganges):** A high-speed river rafting sequence. The player must steer the wooden raft, dodging whirlpools, falling rocks, and water crocodiles, guided by Guha.
*   **Defeat Condition:** Successfully crossing the river and entering the forest boundary, leaving royal life behind.

---

## 6. Script & Dialogue
*   **Kaikeyi:** *"If your father's word is law, Rama, then prove it. Strip off your royal silks, wear the bark of the forest, and do not show your face for fourteen years."*
*   **Rama (bowing):** *"Mother, I go gladly. A kingdom is small compared to the honor of my father's promise. Keep Ayodhya safe, and let Bharata rule with justice."*
*   **Lakshmana (furious):** *"This is treason! Rama, let me draw my blade. We can seize this palace in a heartbeat!"*
*   **Rama:** *"Quiet, Lakshmana. True victory lies in conquering our anger, not our father's throne."*

---

## 7. Costumes & Clothing
*   **Rama, Lakshmana, Sita (Royal -> Ascetic transition):**
    *   *Beginning:* Ornate royal silk dhotis, crown, and pearl necklaces.
    *   *Mid-Act:* In a highly emotional cutscene, they strip their silks, wearing coarse orange tree-bark (*Valkala*). Hair is tied in a rough ascetic knot (*Jata-mukuta*).
*   **Guha:** Simple fisherman's wrap, rope belts, and shell neckpieces.

## 8. Asset & Build Requirements

### Art Assets
*   **3D Models:** King Dasharatha (ailing), Kaikeyi (stern), Manthara (humpbacked, plotting), Guha, Wooden Raft.
*   **Environments:** Ayodhya Palace inner chambers (dim torches, drapes), Ganges riverbed (muddy banks, tall reeds), Chitrakoot cottage (green forest, bamboo, leaf-huts).
*   **Particle Effects:** Mist over the Ganges, sparkling river water, falling autumn leaves in Chitrakoot.

### Audio Assets
*   *Soundtrack:* Melancholic flute and solo veena for the exile departure; rhythmic rowing chants for the Ganges crossing; peaceful bansuri and sitar for Chitrakoot.
*   *SFX:* Crowds weeping, river splashing, wooden logs creaking, axe chopping wood (ashram build).

### Level Design Elements
*   Dialogue choice branching UI.
*   River steering mechanics with raft health bar.
*   "Hermitage Build" interactive construction nodes.
