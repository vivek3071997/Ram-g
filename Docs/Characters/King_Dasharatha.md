## 2. Combat & Gameplay Properties

### Powers & Special Abilities
*   **Shabda-Bhedi Vidya (Sound-Targeting):** Dasharatha is one of the very few mortals to master sound-sniping. In flashback combat tutorials, he can lock onto and strike hidden targets behind solid cover using only acoustic feedback.
*   **Royal Decree (Sun-Ward):** Casts a wide-area defensive zone that increases the armor and posture of all Ayodhya palace guards by 50% and reduces demon infiltration stealth rates.
*   **Chariot Mastery:** In narrative chariot defense sequences (Act 1), Dasharatha can drive his war chariot through demon infantry ranks, creating massive crushing lanes.

### Weapons & Divine Astras
*   **Ikshvaku Bow:** A majestic gold-plated recurve bow that shoots heavy steel arrows.
*   **Solar Blade:** A golden broadsword used for sweeping slashes during close-quarters defense.

## 3. Narrative & Objectives

### Biography
Dasharatha is the king of Kosala, ruling from its capital Ayodhya. Descended from the legendary Solar dynasty (Ikshvaku), he was a mighty conqueror whose chariots could ride in ten directions (earning him his name). He fought alongside Indra against demon forces, during which Queen Kaikeyi saved his life, earning two boons. In his old age, after a horse sacrifice (*Putrakameshti Yajna*), he was blessed with four sons: Rama, Lakshmana, Bharata, and Shatrughna. When Kaikeyi demanded the exile of Rama and the crowning of Bharata, Dasharatha was bound by his ancient boons. Unable to bear the separation, he passed away from a broken heart shortly after Rama's departure, fulfilling a curse laid upon him in his youth by the blind parents of Shravana Kumar.

### Core Objectives
*   **Act 1 (The Gurukul Request):** Act as the royal quest giver, reluctantly permitting young Rama and Lakshmana to accompany Sage Vishwamitra to defend the forest sacrifices.
*   **Act 2 (The Royal Coronation):** Announce the crown-prince coronation, leading the player through a majestic city-building exploration quest.
*   **Act 3 (The Exile Crisis):** Triggers the tragic exile cinematic where the player, unable to fight the palace laws, must say farewell to the weeping king, leading to his narrative demise.

## 4. Audio & Acoustic Design

### Musical Theme & Soundtrack
*   **Acoustic Style:** Grand but mournful classical theme featuring royal Shehnai oboes, solemn Veena strings, slow-tempo temple bells, and a sorrowful solo female classical vocal melody.
*   **Melodic Tempo & Key:** Very slow, tragic tempo, set in Raga Darbari (traditional scale conveying royalty, solemnity, and crushing grief), written in C minor.
*   **Integration:** Palace coronation prep plays bright, major-key Shehnai tunes; his exile dialogues and passing drop into a silent, echo-heavy cello and Sarangi lament.

### Sound Effects (SFX)
*   **Footstep Impacts:** Heavy, slow thuds of leather sandals on polished marble floors, accompanied by the rustle of royal silk robes and the clinking of heavy golden necklaces.
*   **Vocalizations & Grunts:** Trembling, elderly baritone sighs; painful, deep gasps under emotional duress; clear, warm fatherly blessings.
*   **Combat Action Sounds:** Firing the Ikshvaku bow produces a heavy, metallic "clang"; war chariot wheels rumble with thunderous iron vibrations.

## 5. Visual & Particle Effects (VFX)

### Passive Appearance Effects
*   **Passive Aura:** A faint, fading golden crown halo (representing the waning Solar light of his life-force) coiling around his shoulders.
*   **Environmental Interaction:** Polished marble floors reflect his glowing sandals; falling gold leaf particles drift downward around his throne during royal assemblies.

### Active Combat Particles
*   **Ability Activation:** Expansions of bright sandstone-yellow light waves; glowing Sanskrit runes of "Dharma" and "Satya" (Truth) coiling around his bow during archery shots.
*   **Damage Impact Feedback:** Impact of his arrows creates brief bursts of gold dust and stone shards, cracking the ground beneath target impact spots.
