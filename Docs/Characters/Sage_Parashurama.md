## 2. Combat & Gameplay Properties

### Powers & Special Abilities
*   **Lightning Parry (Vajra Parry):** Parashurama stands in a meditative parry stance. Any melee or ranged attack blocked in this state instantly triggers a lightning counter-strike, dealing 150% damage and stunning the attacker.
*   **Tapas Aura (Spiritual Shield):** A passive ward generated by his immense penance (*Tapas*). Reduces incoming magic, projectile, and status-effect damage by 75%.
*   **Earth-Shatter Stamp:** Stomps the ground, causing high-damage fissures to ripple outward in three lines, knocking up enemies and breaking their block state.

### Weapons & Divine Astras
*   **The Parashu Axe:** A massive celestial battleaxe gifted by Lord Shiva. Each swing deals heavy armor-shredding damage, producing razor-sharp lightning shockwaves.
*   **The Sharanga Bow:** A colossal iron bow used to test Lord Rama's strength, firing high-density solar arrows.

## 3. Narrative & Objectives

### Biography
Parashurama is the sixth incarnation of Lord Vishnu, born to the sage Jamadagni and Renuka of the Bhrigu clan. Blessed with invincibility and armed with Lord Shiva's divine axe (Parashu), he rid the world of corrupt, tyrannical kings twenty-one times after they murdered his father. In Mithila, when Lord Rama strings and breaks the sacred Pinaka bow (Lord Shiva's bow), Parashurama arrives in a furious storm of thunder and lightning, demanding to duel the one who broke the bow. Confronting Rama, he realizes that Rama is the seventh avatar of Vishnu. He surrenders his divine bow, blesses Rama, and retires to Mount Mahendra for eternal penance.

### Core Objectives
*   **Act 2 (The Storm of Mithila):** Act as the ultimate post-wedding trial boss. The player must survive his lightning parry phases and dual axe combos in the courtyards of Mithila.
*   **Act 2 (The Bow Test):** The player, controlling Lord Rama, must string the giant Sharanga Bow handed by Parashurama, completing a high-difficulty Quick-Time Event (QTE).
*   **Act 2 (Blessing of Mount Mahendra):** Yield the battlefield, pass his divine weapons and blessings to Lord Rama, and withdraw to Mount Mahendra.

## 4. Audio & Acoustic Design

### Musical Theme & Soundtrack
*   **Acoustic Style:** Terrifying, high-energy Vedic orchestra featuring thunderous war drums (Dundubhi), screaming bronze trumpets, rapid Veena plucking, and a fierce, roaring Sanskrit choir chanting martial mantras.
*   **Melodic Tempo & Key:** Frantic, driving tempo set in Raga Bhairav (martial Rudra-Tandava style), written in a dark E minor scale.
*   **Integration:** His entrance triggers a deafening thunderclap that overrides the wedding chimes, transitioning into a chaotic, adrenaline-fueled combat arrangement.

### Sound Effects (SFX)
*   **Footstep Impacts:** Rock-crushing thuds accompanied by crackling static lightning discharges; rustle of rough tiger-skin wrap.
*   **Vocalizations & Grunts:** Booming, thunderous voice that shakes the screen; short, furious battle growls; deep, resonant meditative breathing.
*   **Combat Action Sounds:** Axe swings produce high-pitched lightning crackles and metal shearing swooshes; parrying an attack generates a metallic clanging blast resembling a thunderbolt.

## 5. Visual & Particle Effects (VFX)

### Passive Appearance Effects
*   **Passive Aura:** Arcs of crackling blue and white lightning discharging from his massive axe and coiling around his arms and shoulders.
*   **Environmental Interaction:** The soil beneath him chars and sparks with blue electricity; wind-torn dust devils swirl around his feet.

### Active Combat Particles
*   **Ability Activation:** Blinding bolts of lightning striking from the sky onto his axe, creating an expanding blue electromagnetic field; giant golden-yellow geometric runes flashing when he parries.
*   **Damage Impact Feedback:** Exploding bursts of electric blue sparks and jagged shock fragments expanding on impact, leaving small sizzling trails in the air.
