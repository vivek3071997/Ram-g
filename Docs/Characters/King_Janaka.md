## 2. Combat & Gameplay Properties

### Powers & Special Abilities
*   **Mithila Elemental Wards:** Janaka is the keeper of the Pinaka bow's puzzle seals. The player must interact with Janaka to align elemental stone conduits (Earth, Water, Fire) in the Mithila courtyard, unlocking the chamber of the giant bow.
*   **Philosophy Buff (Rajarishi Focus):** Interacting with Janaka before trials increases the player's parry window and focus charge rates by 25% for the next 30 minutes.
*   **Earth Detection:** As the one who discovered Sita in the soil, Janaka has an active passive that reveals hidden underground chests and quest items in Mithila's territories.

### Weapons & Divine Astras
*   **Scepter of Mithila:** A heavy golden staff with crystal tips, used to direct elemental streams during palace puzzle sequences.
*   **Videha Shield:** Casts a reflective crystal barrier that deflects magical missiles back at attacking demons.

## 3. Narrative & Objectives

### Biography
Janaka is the king of Videha, ruling from his capital Mithila. He is celebrated in Vedic scriptures as a *Rajarishi* (a king who is also a fully enlightened sage), possessing absolute spiritual wisdom while carrying out royal duties. While ploughing a sacrificial field as part of a royal ritual, he discovered a baby girl in a golden casket inside the earth furrow and named her Sita (furrow). Janaka announced that only the warrior who could lift and string the massive, celestial bow Pinaka (given by Lord Shiva) would win Sita's hand. After many kings failed, young Lord Rama lifted and broke the bow, fulfilling the test.

### Core Objectives
*   **Act 2 (The Court of Mithila):** Act as the royal puzzle anchor. Guide the player through palace explorations to collect and align the three sacred elemental crystals.
*   **Act 2 (The Pinaka Trial Introduction):** Lead Rama and Sage Vishwamitra to the heavy iron vault on eight wheels containing the Pinaka, triggering the grand stringing challenge.
*   **Act 2 (The Royal Marriage):** Hand Sita over to Rama in a grand cinematic, sealing the divine alliance of Ayodhya and Mithila.

## 4. Audio & Acoustic Design

### Musical Theme & Soundtrack
*   **Acoustic Style:** Serene, majestic, and crystalline classical theme featuring solo Santoor (hammered dulcimer), soft Veena strings, deep wooden flutes, and tranquil temple chimes.
*   **Melodic Tempo & Key:** Slow, peaceful, set in Raga Yaman (representing purity, peace, and spiritual wisdom), written in a bright C# Major scale.
*   **Integration:** Mithila's exploration and puzzle segments feature ambient Santoor loops and soft wind chimes; his dialogue drops the background track into a silent, resonant drone.

### Sound Effects (SFX)
*   **Footstep Impacts:** Quiet sandaled clicks on smooth white marble floor tiles; soft rustling of white silk robes.
*   **Vocalizations & Grunts:** Serene, elderly baritone voice speaking with absolute calm; slow, deep breathing; comforting, wise laughter.
*   **Combat Action Sounds:** Directing scepter beams produces resonant, glass-ringing hums; crystal shields create sharp, high-pitched chime crackles when struck.
