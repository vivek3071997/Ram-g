## 2. Propulsion & Hydrofoil Mechanics

*   **Silent MHD Drive:** The boat draws river water into a central insulated channel. Superconducting magnets create an intense electromagnetic field within the water channel, and electrodes apply a voltage. This generates a Lorentz force that drives the water backward, propelling the skiff forward with zero mechanical vibration.
*   **Hydrofoil Lift (Foil Flight):** At low speeds, the hull floats normally. Once speed exceeds `15 knots`, the underwater foil wings generate upward lift vectors. As the hull rises out of the water, hydrodynamic friction drops by `75%`, allowing the skiff to glide smoothly over Godavari and Ganges rapids with minimum fuel consumption.

## 3. Visual & Audio Design

### Visual Particles
*   **Hydrofoil Spray:** At high speeds, twin, highly focused spray sheets of clean river water erupt off the underwater foil wings, with zero bubbly wake trails from the stern.
*   **Hydrogen Exhaust Vapor:** A tiny, wispy trail of cool water vapor dissipates instantly from the silent stern vents.

### Sound SFX
*   **Silent Gliding:** The soft, relaxing sound of water splashing against carbon foils (*"ssshhh-washhh"*), with absolute zero diesel engine or propeller noise.
*   **MHD Hum:** A very faint, high-frequency electrical hum (*"mmmmmmmm"*) only audible within a `2m` radius.
*   **Rapid Crossings:** The crisp, sudden slap of waves against the Kevlar hull plates during river rapid traversals.
