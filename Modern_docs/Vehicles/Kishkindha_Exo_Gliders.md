# Kishkindha Gliders (The Cliff Hang-Gliders)

*   **Vehicle Class:** High-Mobility Aerodynamic Hang-Gliders & winched Climber Assemblies (Air / Traversal Class)
*   **Primary Wielders:** Sugriva's Scout Corps (The Varanas) and Lakshmana
*   **Narrative Function:** Vertical traversal of Kishkindha's deep stone canyons, airborne scouting, and high-altitude assaults on Vali's stronghold.

---

## 1. Technical & Aerodynamic Specifications

The Kishkindha Gliders are rigid-wing carbon-composite hang-gliders designed specifically for high-velocity launches from cliff edges. They feature a high lift-to-drag ratio and are equipped with a mechanical micro-winch climber harness, supporting rapid vertical mobility.

| Parameter | Metric Value | Aerospace / Mechanical Rationale |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Wing Material** | Carbon-Composite Frame / Ripstop Nylon | Provides extreme lightweight durability and structural yield strength. |
| **Wing Design** | High-Aspect Rigid Swept-Wing Profile | Optimizes glide efficiency and lateral stability in canyon crosswinds. |
| **Lift-to-Drag Ratio**| 14 : 1 | For every `1m` of drop, the glider travels `14m` horizontally. |
| **Wing Area** | 8.5 $m^2$ | Provides exceptional lift capacity for pilot + heavy tactical gear. |
| **Total Weight** | 18 kg (Glider Frame Only) | Extremely portable; can be folded and slung over shoulders. |
| **Winch Core** | High-Tensile Steel Wire Winch | Battery-powered micro-spool supports rapid vertical ascends at `5 m/s`. |
| **Anchoring Claw** | Compressed-Gas Grapple Hook | Launches anchoring lines up to `40 meters` onto rock ledges. |

---

## 2. Flight & Ascent Mechanics

*   **Canyon Gliding Phase:** Launched by sprinting off vertical cliff edges. The pilot controls pitch and roll vectors by shifting their physical body mass on a carbon-fiber control frame, or warping the flexible ripstop nylon wing tips.
*   **Thermal Updraft Utilization:** Canyon topography naturally generates intense thermal updrafts during daylight hours. Pilots map these warm air currents on their HUD, riding them to maintain altitude or climb without propulsion.
*   **Rapid Winch Ascension:** In deep caverns, pilots deploy the gas-compressed grapple claw onto overhead metal scaffolds or rock crevices. Once anchored, the high-torque winch spool ascends the pilot vertically, bypassing difficult climbing walls.

## 3. Visual & Audio Design

### Visual Particles
*   **Canyon Air Dust:** Glide sweeps close to canyon floors kick up small dust whirlwinds and dry leaves conforming to down-wash wing vectors.
*   **Vapor Tip Trails:** In humid conditions, faint white tip-vortices condense off the swept-back wing tips.

### Sound SFX
*   **Aerodynamic Rush:** The relaxing, high-frequency hiss of wind rushing past the nylon wing membranes (*"shhhhh-faaa"*).
*   **Grapple Hook Blast:** The sudden, loud pneumatic pop (*"thwip-PANG"*) of the compressed-gas grapple hook launching.
*   **Winch Ascent:** The rapid, high-pitched mechanical hum (*"ziiiiiiip"*) of the high-torque electric spool winding.
