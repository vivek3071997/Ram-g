## 2. Combat & Gameplay Properties

### Powers & Special Abilities
*   **The Two Boons (Dialogue Boss Mechanic):** Kaikeyi does not engage in physical combat. Instead, she acts as a high-difficulty "Dialogue Boss" in the chambers of Ayodhya. The player must select precise diplomatic, Dharmic, and emotional dialogue choices to mitigate her demands. However, her "Two Boons" act as an absolute shield that overrides logical palace arguments.
*   **Palace Manipulation:** She is immune to standard charms, arguments, or intimidation. Her whispers reduce the confidence and focus gauge of the interlocutor by 30%.
*   **Court Veto:** When active, she can lock down player movements in the palace sector, forcing them to obey the royal exile decree.

### Weapons & Divine Astras
*   **Royal Decree:** Her primary weapon. A written scroll that can seal areas of the palace, blocking access to royal vaults.
*   **Scepter of Kekeya:** A small golden scepter used to direct palace guards.

## 3. Narrative & Objectives

### Biography
Kaikeyi is the favorite queen of King Dasharatha and the princess of Kekeya (modern Pakistan/Afghanistan border). A skilled warrior, she once drove Dasharatha's chariot in battle against demons, saving his life when he was wounded. In gratitude, Dasharatha granted her two boons, which she kept in reserve. On the eve of Rama's coronation, Kaikeyi's wicked wet-nurse Manthara poisoned her mind, convincing her that Bharata would be enslaved if Rama became king. Driven by fear and ambition, Kaikeyi demanded her two boons: that Bharata be crowned king, and that Rama be exiled to the Dandaka forest for 14 years. Dasharatha complied and died of grief. Later, Bharata severely rebuked her, and she lived in deep repentance.

### Core Objectives
*   **Act 2 (The Chariot Flashback):** Control Kaikeyi in a flashback sequence, driving Dasharatha's chariot through demon hordes, dodging fireballs to save the king.
*   **Act 3 (The Chamber of Anger):** Act as the dialogue boss. The player, playing as Dasharatha, must navigate a tense, heart-wrenching debate, trying to save Rama from exile while Kaikeyi relentlessly deploys her boons.
*   **Act 10 (Royal Forgiveness):** Welcome Rama back to Ayodhya, falling at his feet in tears, and receiving his warm, compassionate forgiveness.

## 4. Audio & Acoustic Design

### Musical Theme & Soundtrack
*   **Acoustic Style:** Elegant but highly tense and dramatic classical theme featuring sharp Sitar plucks, high-pitched violin loops, heavy wooden shakers, and a sorrowful female vocal line.
*   **Melodic Tempo & Key:** Moderate, slow, set in Raga Lalit (evoking dawn, but with a highly unstable, tense key change), written in a dark F minor scale.
*   **Integration:** Initial interactions feature beautiful Sitar loops; the Chamber of Anger duel triggers sharp, dissonant string hits and rapid percussion loops.

### Sound Effects (SFX)
*   **Footstep Impacts:** Sharp clinking of heavy golden anklets on polished stone floor tiles; soft rustling of silk saris.
*   **Vocalizations & Grunts:** Commanding, sharp, high-society female voice; breathy, proud sighs; trembling weeping gasps during emotional breakdowns.
*   **Combat Action Sounds:** The scepter clicking on the floor generates a sharp, metallic "ping"; scrolling decrees make dry paper-tearing sounds.
