## 2. Combat & Gameplay Properties

### Powers & Special Abilities
*   **Astra Awakening:** Vishwamitra acts as the player's core progression trainer. By completing his forest trials, the player (Rama) unlocks new celestial weapons (Agneyastra, Varunastra, Vayavyastra).
*   **Tapas Shield:** Casts a spinning wall of fiery orange energy that protects allies from all projectile attacks and burns any demon that touches it.
*   **Bala & Atibala Teachings:** Buffs Rama and Lakshmana's stamina and focus recovery rates by 50%, making them immune to fatigue-based environmental effects in the desert.

### Weapons & Divine Astras
*   **Brahma-Danda (The Sage's Staff):** An indestructible wooden staff that can absorb and reflect any magical spell or boss arrow targeted at him.
*   **Vedic Chants:** Chanting sacred mantras launches concentrated bolts of white cosmic light that dissolve minor demon shadows.

## 3. Narrative & Objectives

### Biography
Vishwamitra was originally a powerful king (Kaushika) who sought to conquer Sage Vashishta's wish-fulfilling cow Kamadhenu. Defeated by Vashishta's spiritual power, the humiliated king realized that spiritual penance was greater than physical weapons. He renounced his kingdom and performed intense penance for thousands of years, eventually achieving the state of *Brahmarishi*. In the epic, when his forest sacrifices were repeatedly desecrated by Tataka, Maricha, and Subahu, Vishwamitra came to Ayodhya, requesting young Rama and Lakshmana's protection, teaching them combat, and introducing them to Sita in Mithila.

### Core Objectives
*   **Act 1 (The Forest Gurukul):** Guide the player through combat tutorials, teaching the basic archery mechanics, the use of basic tools, and stealth movement.
*   **Act 1 (Tataka Trial):** Direct Rama to slay the demoness Tataka, overcoming Rama's hesitation about fighting a female by teaching the priority of Dharma.
*   **Act 2 (Mithila Swayamvara):** Accompany the princes to Mithila, acting as the narrative facilitator who guides Rama to lift the legendary Pinaka bow.

## 4. Audio & Acoustic Design

### Musical Theme & Soundtrack
*   **Acoustic Style:** Mysterious, spiritual, and powerful theme featuring classical Veena, rich bamboo flutes, resonant wind-chimes, and high-frequency temple bells layered with deep, slow chanting of Vedic hymns.
*   **Melodic Tempo & Key:** Moderate, slow, set in Raga Bhairav (representing early morning prayer and spiritual discipline), written in a bright A minor.
*   **Integration:** Forest exploration sequences play soft bamboo flutes and ambient forest rustles; his training sequences trigger soaring strings and resonant, mystical choir loops.

### Sound Effects (SFX)
*   **Footstep Impacts:** Soft, grass-stepping steps; the clicking of a wooden staff on rocks; ambient water splashes from his copper water pot.
*   **Vocalizations & Grunts:** Commanding, resonant, elder baritone voice; sharp breathing during combat spells; rhythmic Sanskrit incantations.
*   **Combat Action Sounds:** The Brahma-Danda produces a high-tension electrical hum when absorbing attacks; Vedic chants create ringing glass-chime sounds upon impact.

## 5. Visual & Particle Effects (VFX)

### Passive Appearance Effects
*   **Passive Aura:** Spinning circular Sanskrit runes of glowing red and orange energy coiling around his staff and wrists when standing idle.
*   **Environmental Interaction:** Wild forest creatures gather near him peacefully; dry leaves on the ground gently rise and spin around his path.

### Active Combat Particles
*   **Ability Activation:** Giant expanding circles of bright orange solar flame coiling outwards; glowing golden-yellow Sanskrit mantras floating around trained targets.
*   **Damage Impact Feedback:** Exploding bursts of cosmic white stars and gold dust upon impact, leaving trailing rings of Vedic light that fade slowly.
