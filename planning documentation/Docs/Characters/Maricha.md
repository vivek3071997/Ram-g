## 2. Combat & Gameplay Properties

### Powers & Special Abilities
*   **Golden Decoy (Multi-Deer Phase):** Maricha creates 4 perfect illusory duplicates of the golden deer that run in multiple directions. The player (Rama) must use his *Shabda-Bhedi Vidya* focus mode to identify which deer emits a real physical heartbeat sound, tracking the correct target through the dense trees of Panchavati.
*   **Sound Mimicry (Voice Clone):** When shot with Rama's fatal arrow, Maricha uses his last breath to mimic Rama's exact vocalization, crying out "Ah Sita! Ah Lakshmana!" in Rama's voice. This triggers the narrative event that lures Lakshmana away from the cottage.
*   **Vayu Sprint (Dash):** Can perform rapid zig-zag leaps, leaving trailing mirages of gold dust to confuse targeted archery shots.

### Weapons & Divine Astras
*   **Illusion Horns:** Sharp, crystal-like golden antlers used to charge and headbutt the player, causing temporary stun and vision distortion.
*   **Hoof-Strike:** Rapid kick combo used to deflect incoming frontal arrow projects.

## 3. Narrative & Objectives

### Biography
Maricha is the son of the demoness Tataka and the brother of Subahu. In his youth, he desecrated the sacrifices of Sage Vishwamitra until young Rama shot him with a cold weapon (the Manavastra), throwing him a hundred yojanas into the ocean. Surviving the strike, a deeply traumatized Maricha reformed and lived as a quiet ascetic in the forest, terrified of Rama's name. When Ravana plotted to kidnap Sita, he forced Maricha under threat of death to transform into a beautiful golden deer to lure Rama away from the cottage. Maricha complied, knowing he would die by Rama's hand, which he considered a path to liberation.

### Core Objectives
*   **Act 5 (The Golden Chase):** Guide the player through a high-speed chase sequence in the forest, dodging traps, bounding over gorges, and shooting gold decoys.
*   **Act 5 (The Sniping Shot QTE):** The player, controlling Rama, must complete a precision arrow-draw sequence to strike Maricha through a small clearing in the dense canopy.
*   **Act 5 (The Tragic Death Cry):** Triggers the cinematic where Maricha returns to his demonic form, screams the voice-cloned cry, and achieves liberation under Rama's gaze.

## 4. Audio & Acoustic Design

### Musical Theme & Soundtrack
*   **Acoustic Style:** Frantic, high-velocity chase theme featuring Santoor, classical bamboo flute, running Tablas, and shimmering wind chimes, transitioning into a dark, tense orchestral hit upon his death cry.
*   **Melodic Tempo & Key:** Double-tempo, energetic scale, written in Raga Jog (evoking chasing, mystery, and tragic urgency), played in B minor.
*   **Integration:** The chase features upbeat woodwinds and Santoor loops that accelerate as the distance between Rama and the deer decreases; the death scene drops out all instruments, leaving only the echoing cloned cry.

### Sound Effects (SFX)
*   **Footstep Impacts:** Rapid, crisp hoof clicks on soil and dry forest leaves; soft rustling bushes.
*   **Vocalizations & Grunts:** Gentle deer grunts; sudden terrified gasps; perfect vocal copy of Rama's voice during his final scream.
*   **Combat Action Sounds:** Leaping generates whistling wind dashes; arrow impacts sound like heavy thuds followed by evaporating purple smoke sizzles.

## 5. Visual & Particle Effects (VFX)

### Passive Appearance Effects
*   **Passive Aura:** Shimmering gold and silver sparkles and tiny glowing dust motes floating off his fur when running.
*   **Environmental Interaction:** Glowing hoof-tracks sprout temporary golden grass blades that fade into light after 3 seconds.

### Active Combat Particles
*   **Ability Activation:** Expansions of bright gold light and duplicate deer shadows coiling outwards; glowing silver runes flashing on the target deer.
*   **Damage Impact Feedback:** Exploding bursts of golden sparkles and purple shadow mist upon arrow strike, returning to his true dark demonic form.
