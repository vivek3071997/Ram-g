## 1. Tactical & Engineering Specifications

The Kodanda is a high-performance sporting recurve designed with advanced carbon-fiber composites and aerospace magnesium-aluminum alloys, delivering extreme kinetic energy transfer.

| Parameter | Metric Value | Engineering / Physics Rationale |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Limb Material** | High-Modulus Carbon Fiber / Maple Core | Maximizes elastic potential energy storage and reduces weight. |
| **Riser Material** | T6 Aircraft-Grade Magnesium-Aluminum Alloy | Provides exceptional torsional stiffness, preventing riser twist during heavy draws. |
| **Max Draw Weight** | 90 lbs (Adjustable from 65 lbs) | Generates `120 Joules` of kinetic energy at release. |
| **Muzzle Velocity** | 95 m/s (311 ft/s) | Flawless flat trajectory up to `50 meters`, minimizing wind drift. |
| **Kinetic Efficiency**| 88% | High energy transfer from limb elasticity to arrow shaft, minimizing vibration loss. |

| **Targeting HUD** | Holographic Optic Sight | Projects real-time wind speed, arrow drop curve, and distance metrics on Rama's visor. |

*   **Base Kinetic Damage:** 150 HP.
*   **Effective Range:** 180 meters (Near-zero gravity trajectory falloff).

Drawing the string triggers a high-intensity, solid-state laser bolt (condensed light shaft) that draws power from a shoulder-mounted solar accumulator pack. Shafts pierce multiple lined targets.

## 4. Acoustic Profile & Sound Design

### Sound Effects (SFX)
*   **Tension Drawing:** The tight, metallic strain of the carbon limbs bending under high draw weight; the micro-frictional slide of the composite string across the rest.
*   **Arrow Release:** A loud, clean, high-frequency string snap (*"Twang-SHHH"*), layered with the aerodynamic whistle of the arrow cutting through air.
*   **Target Collision:** A solid, low-frequency bone-cracking *TOCK* on armor or wood; a sharp, high-frequency kinetic hiss when energy shafts vaporize targets.
